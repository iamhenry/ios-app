# Caption Guide

How to write Instagram captions that complement the Reel and drive engagement.

The caption is the second viral mechanism — the Reel stops the scroll, the caption converts
attention into action (follow, save, share, comment, download the app).

## Caption Structure

Every caption follows this 4-part structure:

### 1. Hook Line (first 125 characters)

Instagram truncates captions at ~125 characters in the feed. Everything after that requires
tapping "...more." The hook line must be compelling enough to earn that tap — or strong
enough to stand alone if nobody taps.

**Patterns that work:**

- **Continuation of the Reel's message:** "The hardest part isn't quitting. It's realizing
  you don't miss it."
- **Question that the Reel answered visually:** "When did you first realize alcohol wasn't
  adding anything?"
- **Bold restatement:** "This changed how I think about every Friday night."
- **Social proof:** "47 people sent this to someone today. Be the 48th."

**What doesn't work:**
- Restating exactly what the Reel already said (redundant)
- Generic motivational language ("You got this!" without specificity)
- Starting with hashtags or emojis (algorithm deprioritizes)

### 2. Body (2-4 sentences)

Expand on the content's message. This is where you add depth that the Reel (which is
usually under 10 seconds) can't fit.

**Match the emotional register of the Reel:**
- If the Reel is humorous (T1 Meme) → the body can be light, relatable, conversational
- If the Reel is serious (T4 Truth Bomb) → the body should be direct, factual, no-nonsense
- If the Reel is aspirational (T3 Text Card) → the body should validate and encourage
- If the Reel is premium/functional (P1) → the body can be minimal or instructional

**Read `soul.md` (in this same references directory) before writing.** The body must sound like the brand voice.

### 3. Call-to-Action

The CTA drives a specific behavior. Choose ONE per post — multiple CTAs dilute each other.

**CTA types ranked by algorithmic value:**

1. **DM share CTA** — "Send this to someone who needs to hear it today"
   - Drives DM shares, which is Instagram's #2 ranking signal (per Adam Mosseri, Jan 2025)
   - Best for: T1 Meme, T2 Quote Card (highly shareable formats)

2. **Comment keyword CTA** — "Comment ZEROPROOF for your free sobriety toolkit"
   - Drives comments + builds a lead list via automation (ManyChat or similar)
   - Best for: any format, especially for accounts with <10K followers building a list

3. **Save CTA** — "Save this for day 30"
   - Drives saves, which signal content value to the algorithm
   - Best for: T3 Text Card, T4 Truth Bomb, P3 Cultural Edutainment (reference-worthy content)

4. **Engagement CTA** — "What's your number? Drop your days in the comments"
   - Drives comments through personal sharing
   - Best for: T2 Quote Card, milestone-related content

5. **Link CTA** — "Link in bio to start tracking"
   - Drives app downloads but doesn't help algorithmic reach
   - Use sparingly — only when the Reel's content directly relates to the app's function

### 4. Hashtags (max 5)

Place hashtags after the CTA, separated by a line break. Instagram's recommendation
(as of 2025) is 3-5 targeted hashtags, not 20-30 broad ones.

**Hashtag strategy:**
- 2 niche-specific: terms your target audience actively searches (#sobercurious #greyareadrinker)
- 1 community: a tag the niche community rallies around (#soberlife #alcoholfree)
- 1 content-type: describes the format (#sobrietytips #sobermotivation)
- 1 trending/seasonal: if applicable (#dryanuary #sobersummer)

Do not use hashtags that are too broad (#motivation, #wellness) — they dilute reach
into audiences that aren't your target.

## Caption Examples by Format Tier

### T1 (Meme Reel)
```
The journey is... exactly like this. Every single day.

90 days in and I still feel like that kayaker sometimes.
But at least I remember every wave now.

Send this to someone who gets it.

#soberlife #sobercurious #sobrietyhumor #recoveryjourney
```

### T2 (Quote Card)
```
Read that again.

The best time to start was years ago.
The second best time is right now.
Every day you wait is a day you could've had.

Comment ZEROPROOF if today is your day 1.

#sober #quitdrinking #sobercurious #alcoholfree
```

### T3 (Text Card)
```
Sobriety isn't what they told you it would be.

It's not about what you're giving up.
It's about everything you're finally letting in.
The list is longer than you think.

Save this for the days it feels hard.

#soberlife #sobriety #alcoholfreelifestyle #sobercurious
```

### T4 (Truth Bomb)
```
Nobody talks about this.

We normalize a substance that causes cancer,
then question the people who choose not to consume it.
That's not health culture. That's marketing.

What changed your mind? Drop it below.

#sobercurious #alcoholfree #sobermovement #quitdrinking
```

### P3 (Cultural Edutainment)
```
Your new favorite word.

Some languages have words for feelings we've all had
but never named. This one hits different if you've
ever wondered why "normal" drinking felt so heavy.

Save this. You'll want to come back to it.

#sobervocabulary #sobercurious #alcoholfree #soberlife
```

## Formatting Rules

- No emojis in the hook line (they reduce perceived seriousness for wellness/sobriety niche)
- Emojis OK sparingly in the body or CTA (max 1-2 per caption)
- Line breaks between sections for readability
- Never start with a hashtag
- Never use more than 5 hashtags
- Match the caption's emotional tone to the Reel's emotional tone — tonal mismatch confuses the audience
