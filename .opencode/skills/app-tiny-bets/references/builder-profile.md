# Tiny Bets Builder Profile

Use this profile as a soft prior for opportunity discovery. It describes the builder's recurring product taste and practical constraints without acting as an idea inventory. Market evidence always outranks profile alignment.

## Builder Context

- Independent developer building a portfolio of small, profitable apps rather than one venture-scale company.
- Prefers tracer bullets: prove one complete user outcome quickly, then expand only when usage supports it.
- Defaults to iOS for App Store distribution. Mac, browser extensions, plugins, and lightweight web companions are credible when they are the natural surface for the job.
- Values products that can become durable, low-overhead income without a large team, sales operation, or content organization.
- Often starts from repeated firsthand friction or a problem observed in a specific person's real workflow, then looks for broader market proof.

## Audiences With Natural Pull

Look first for narrow problems experienced by:

- Consumers improving focus, habits, wellbeing, reflection, or everyday organization.
- Readers, listeners, learners, and hobbyists who want better discovery, curation, retention, or progress tracking.
- Individuals and families preserving personal memories or reducing friction in everyday shared life.
- Creators who repeatedly turn rough material into useful or publishable content.
- Independent builders, designers, and developers removing friction from a repeated workflow.
- Knowledge workers overwhelmed by inputs, updates, feedback, or fragmented tools.

These are affinity signals, not fixed market boundaries. A better-supported audience should win.

## Product Mechanics That Fit

Strong opportunities often use one of these compact loops:

- Capture -> transform -> useful result: accept a photo, voice note, text, URL, or file and produce one clear output.
- Detect -> explain -> act: identify something important, explain it simply, and offer a safe next step.
- Import -> organize -> surface: reduce a messy collection into prioritized, searchable, or timely information.
- Track -> visualize -> reinforce: make progress tangible through a timeline, streak, collection, or meaningful visual reward.
- Select -> personalize -> generate: use a few deliberate choices to create an output suited to the user's context.
- Observe -> personalize -> recommend: use the user's own history, preferences, or activity to surface a timely next step.
- Monitor -> notify -> resolve: watch a narrow condition and alert the user when action is worthwhile.
- Convert between formats: make existing information easier to consume, retain, share, or reuse.

AI fits when it removes a real transformation step. Narrow agents, bots, and automations also fit when they complete one bounded job with a clear result and recovery path. Avoid generic chat as the product; the user should understand the outcome before invoking AI.

## Product And UX Taste

- One obvious primary action and a short path to first value.
- A non-technical user should understand the promise from the name, screenshots, and first screen.
- Minimal setup; avoid accounts unless sync, billing, or durable history truly requires one.
- Calm, polished, native-feeling interfaces with progress and state made visible.
- User-controlled automation with preview, confirmation, undo, or clear recovery where mistakes could reduce trust.
- Privacy can be a differentiator, especially for personal writing, health-adjacent data, voice, and on-device context. Prefer local processing when it materially improves trust and remains practical.
- Small moments of delight are valuable when they reinforce progress or clarify a state change, not when they delay the task.

## Commercial Shape

Prefer opportunities with:

- A specific App Store search phrase that maps to an obvious job.
- Existing paid competitors or clear evidence that the audience pays for the outcome.
- A useful free first experience and a paid boundary users can understand.
- Recurring value for subscriptions, such as ongoing tracking, monitoring, generation, or fresh insights.
- A lifetime or one-time unlock for occasional utilities, local tools, and finite transformations.
- Adjacent keywords that could support focused follow-up apps or a shared technical foundation.
- Low customer-support, infrastructure, moderation, and content-maintenance burden.

Do not force subscription pricing onto a low-frequency job merely because competitors do.

## Practical Build Filter

A strong fit should be plausible for one developer to validate in weeks, with:

- One core feature that delivers the searched outcome end to end.
- Mature APIs or libraries for difficult inference, media, search, or platform integration.
- Little or no custom backend before the first user receives value.
- A bounded data model and few states to explain or maintain.
- Costs that scale with paid usage or can be capped conservatively.
- A distribution path through App Store search, a platform ecosystem, or a reachable niche audience.

Prefer a narrow wedge into an existing behavior over asking users to adopt an entirely new routine.

## Deprioritize Or Reject

- Marketplaces, social graphs, or communities that need critical mass before becoming useful.
- Broad horizontal AI assistants with many tools, personas, or workflows in the first release.
- Products requiring continuous editorial output or a large proprietary content library.
- Heavy scraping, spam-like automation, policy evasion, or dependence on unstable unofficial access.
- Ticketing, booking, or purchasing automation where errors, abuse controls, or terms of service create high operational risk.
- Medical, legal, financial, or safety claims that require expert-grade accuracy.
- Products whose main advantage is cheaper model access without a differentiated workflow.
- Ideas that need extensive partnerships, enterprise sales, or manual fulfillment to validate.

## How To Apply This Profile

- With no user seed, use the profile as one source in a mixed candidate set that also includes adjacent and profile-independent app families.
- With a broad category seed, explore distinct user jobs and product families from the category first. Then use the profile to add angles, not eliminate options.
- With an explicit app or keyword seed, follow the user's direction. Use the profile only to simplify the MVP, choose monetization, or break close ties.
- Rank profile alignment after demand, competitor proof, payment evidence, and tiny-bet fit.
- Narrow candidates with Astro evidence and the rubric, not with profile fit.
- Do not mention private project history or imply that this profile proves demand.

When evidence is comparable, favor the opportunity with the smaller complete flow, clearer keyword, lower operational burden, stronger trust characteristics, and closer profile fit, in that order.
