**Critical findings from expanded dataset:**

1. **The Quote Card Reel (T2) is the engagement king.** @wisclandscaping's static AI sunset + regret quote got 47.6K likes — the highest in our entire dataset by a wide margin. 7,933 likes per second of content. The format is the simplest possible (static image + text) and the production cost is under 5 minutes.

2. **Under 8 seconds is the sweet spot.** The top 3 performing Reels are all under 8 seconds. The algorithm rewards completion rate, and ultra-short = near-100% completion.

3. **Taste ≠ engagement (but taste = brand).** The Headspace Reel is the most beautifully produced content in our dataset but has the lowest likes-per-second ratio. The @themagicisinthemiddle truth bomb is more elegant than @wisclandscaping's AI sunset but likely gets fewer likes. There's a tension between "what goes viral" and "what builds a premium brand." The skill needs to account for this.

4. **The engagement spectrum:** Regret/urgency > Humor/meme > Provocative truth > Aspirational list > Premium design. Raw virality favors emotional simplicity. Brand building favors design sophistication.

### Recommended Production Ladder for Streaks Zero Proof

**Phase 1 (0-1K followers): T1 + T2 — Maximum reach, minimum production**
- Meme Reels (stock footage + contrasting text, 5 min each)
- Quote Card Reels (AI/stock background + regret/urgency quote, 5 min each)
- Goal: 1 Reel/day, test 3-5 topics per week
- Prioritize: faceless, under 8 seconds, high-shareability quotes

**Phase 2 (1K-10K followers): T3 + T4 — Identity building**
- Text Card Reels (myth vs reality lists, aspirational content)
- Truth Bomb Reels (logical arguments, societal contradiction)
- Goal: 5 Reels/week, start developing visual consistency
- Prioritize: typography system, consistent color palette, niche authority

**Phase 3 (10K+ followers): T5 — Brand elevation**
- Premium Brand Reels (custom illustration, motion design, functional content)
- Borrow Headspace principles: minimal text, looping animations, monochromatic palette
- Example for Streaks: animated day-counter, breathing exercises, milestone celebrations
- Goal: 2-3 premium Reels/week alongside T2-T4 content
- Prioritize: design system, brand mascot/visual identity, content-as-product

### Note for Skill Development: Faceless Priority

When building the viral-research skill, the content discovery and scoring system should:
- **Flag faceless content by default** — detect absence of face/person in thumbnail
- **Categorize into the 5 tiers** based on production complexity and viral mechanism
- **Weight engagement relative to follower count** — 1.9K likes on 2M followers (Headspace) is weak engagement; 47.6K likes on a small account (wisclandscaping) is exceptional
- **Track "taste level" as a separate axis from engagement** — a Reel can score high on taste (Headspace) but low on virality, or vice versa (wisclandscaping). The skill should surface both dimensions

## Pattern Analysis: What the DNA Tells Us

### Hook Patterns (first 1-3 seconds)
All 4 posts use one of two hook strategies:

1. **State the myth as if it's true** — "Going to parties will be less fun" / "I drink because I like it" / "Sobriety is a beautiful journey." This creates cognitive tension. The viewer thinks "wait, is this agreeing with me or challenging me?" That ambiguity stops the scroll.

2. **Frame the contrast upfront** — "What people think sobriety is: vs What sobriety actually is:" This signals value immediately. The viewer knows they're about to learn something and sticks around for the payoff.

**Winner for 6-slide Reels:** Strategy 1 (state the myth) for the hook slide, then reveal the reality on slide 2. This creates a mini-cliffhanger between slides.

### Emotional Triggers
Ranked by frequency across all 4 posts:

1. **Validation** (4/4) — Every post makes the viewer feel understood. "I've been told this my whole life too."
2. **Reframing** (4/4) — Every post takes a negative belief and flips it. "It's not giving something up — it's a power shift."
3. **Identity** (3/4) — The posts speak to who the viewer IS, not just what they do. "A life that feels bigger, not smaller."
4. **Surprise** (2/4) — The neuroscience angle ("prefrontal cortex") and the subverted journey metaphor both deliver unexpected information.

### Text Overlay Style
- **Font:** Bold, high-contrast (white on dark or dark on light)
- **Placement:** Center-weighted for hooks, full-slide for content
- **Hierarchy:** MYTH label in accent color (yellow, bold), FACT in white body text
- **Word count per slide:** 15-30 words max. The @breakfreewithAbhi carousel pushed this limit but stayed readable.
- **Pacing:** Each text block visible for 2-3 seconds in Reels

### CTA Patterns
Three distinct CTA strategies observed:

1. **Question CTA** — "Which myth are you still believing?" (drives comments)
2. **Keyword CTA** — "Comment SOBERCLEAR for free video" (@soberleon, 31.9K likes — highest engagement by far)
3. **Implicit share CTA** — "Share your take" / brand logo at end

**Winner for your account:** Keyword CTA is the most powerful for a small account because it trains the algorithm that your content drives engagement, and it builds a lead list. Consider: "Comment ZEROPROOF for your free sobriety toolkit" or similar.

### Visual Style
- **Dark backgrounds dominate** (3/4 posts use dark/moody tones)
- **Nature footage** works as metaphor (river rapids = turbulent recovery journey)
- **Talking head** works for authority figures but requires on-camera presence
- **Stock/lifestyle photos** with text overlay is the most accessible production format

## 6-Slide Reel Adaptation: Myth vs Reality Format

Based on the DNA analysis, here's how the Myth vs Reality format maps to your 6-slide structure:

### Slide 1 — HOOK
**Purpose:** Stop the scroll. State one provocative myth as if it's true.
**Template:** "[MYTH STATEMENT]" — bold text, center-weighted, dark background
**Example:** "Sobriety means giving up everything fun."
**Duration:** 2-3 seconds
**Design:** Large bold white text on dark/gradient background. No attribution, no logo yet.

### Slide 2 — REALITY REVEAL
**Purpose:** Flip the myth. Deliver the "wait, what?" moment.
**Template:** "Actually... [REFRAME]"
**Example:** "Actually... you're not losing anything. You're finally seeing what was always missing."
**Duration:** 3 seconds
**Design:** Same dark background, but text shifts to a warmer accent color for the reality.

### Slide 3 — EVIDENCE / SPECIFICITY
**Purpose:** Back up the reframe with a specific, concrete detail the audience recognizes.
**Template:** "[SPECIFIC DETAIL THAT PROVES THE REALITY]"
**Example:** "Day 30: You wake up at 6am without an alarm. Your skin is clearing. You remember last night."
**Duration:** 3 seconds
**Design:** Can introduce subtle imagery (sunrise, calm morning aesthetic).

### Slide 4 — EMOTIONAL PAYOFF
**Purpose:** Make the viewer FEEL the transformation. This is the save-worthy slide.
**Template:** "[ASPIRATIONAL IDENTITY STATEMENT]"
**Example:** "That's not deprivation. That's what freedom actually looks like."
**Duration:** 3 seconds
**Design:** Most visually striking slide. Nature imagery or clean typography on gradient.

### Slide 5 — SECOND MYTH OR DEEPENING
**Purpose:** Either introduce a second myth/reality pair OR deepen the emotional resonance.
**Template:** "[ANOTHER COMMON MYTH → QUICK FLIP]" or "[DEEPER TRUTH]"
**Example:** "They told you sobriety is boring. They never told you boredom was just the hangovers talking."
**Duration:** 3 seconds

### Slide 6 — CTA
**Purpose:** Convert attention into action.
**Template:** "[KEYWORD CTA]" + app mention
**Example:** "Comment ZEROPROOF if this hit different. Download Streaks: link in bio."
**Duration:** 3 seconds
**Design:** Clean, minimal. Brand logo + clear action.

### Total Reel Duration: ~15-18 seconds (sweet spot for completion rate)
