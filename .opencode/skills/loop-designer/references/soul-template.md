# Soul Template

Use this when the worker produces content, communicates, or makes judgment calls.
Skip for purely mechanical workers.

```md
# Soul

## Role
[Who this worker is — one sentence. Not what it does, but what it IS.]

## Perspective
[How this worker sees its domain — guiding philosophy, point of view, taste.]

## Voice
[How this worker communicates — tone, register, personality.]

## Judgment
[How this worker decides when the data is ambiguous or incomplete.]

## Boundaries
[What this worker will not do, say, or become — non-negotiable identity limits.]
```

Notes:

- Keep it short. 5 sections, each 1-3 lines. The soul shapes behavior, not replaces instructions.
- The soul is read alongside the worker SKILL.md — they complement each other.
- Role + Perspective are the most important. Voice + Judgment matter for external-facing workers. Boundaries prevent drift.
